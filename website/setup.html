Setup Guide

Build Your BabyBox

Follow this step-by-step guide to go from a bag of parts to a working toddler media player. No prior hardware experience required.

What you need

Setup Steps

Work through each step at your own pace. Most builds take an afternoon.

1

Flash Raspberry Pi OS

  • Download Raspberry Pi OS Lite (64-bit) from the official Raspberry Pi website.
  • Flash the image to your MicroSD card using Raspberry Pi Imager.
  • In the imager's advanced settings, enable SSH and configure your WiFi credentials before writing.
2

Install BabyBox Software

  • SSH into the Pi from your computer using the credentials you configured.
  • Run the one-line installer:
curl -sSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/babybox/install.sh | bash
  • The script installs all dependencies: Python, mpv, Flask, yt-dlp, PulseAudio, RFID libraries, and more.
3

Print the Enclosure & Figurines

  • Print the enclosure (babybox-case.scad) in PETG for durability and toddler-resistance.
  • Print figurine bases (base-template.scad) -- each base has pockets for magnets and an RFID recess.
  • Use Meshy AI to generate fun figurine tops (animals, characters), then merge with the base in Blender to add magnet cavities.
4

Assemble Hardware

  • Wire the MFRC522 RFID module to the Pi's SPI pins.
  • Connect the WS2812 LED strip to GPIO 18.
  • Wire buttons: play/pause to GPIO 17, stop to GPIO 27.
  • Connect the passive buzzer to GPIO 12 (PWM).
  • Insert RFID coin stickers and neodymium magnets into each figurine base.

View Detailed Assembly Guide

5

First Boot & Bluetooth Pairing

  • Power on the Pi via the micro USB power supply.
  • Connect to the web dashboard at http://babybox.local from any device on your network.
  • Go to the settings page and pair your Bluetooth speaker -- all audio routes through PulseAudio to the speaker.
6

Add Your First Content

  • Open the dashboard on your phone at http://babybox.local.
  • Paste a YouTube URL and click download -- choose video (MP4) or audio only (MP3).
  • Place a figurine on the box to scan its RFID tag.
  • Assign the downloaded content to the scanned figurine.
  • Place the figurine again -- it plays!
