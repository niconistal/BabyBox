Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about building and using BabyBox.

Designed for toddlers ages 1-5. The figurine-based interaction requires no reading or screen navigation. Even a 1-year-old can place a figurine on the box.

Absolutely! BabyBox works great as an audio-only player. Just download audio-only content and your child can listen to music, stories, and podcasts through the Bluetooth speaker.

Around $80 for all components. The most expensive parts are the Raspberry Pi (~$15) and a Bluetooth speaker (~$15). If you already have a 3D printer, filament costs about $10.

Any YouTube video or audio. Parents paste YouTube URLs into the dashboard, and the content is downloaded locally. You choose whether to save it as video (MP4) or audio-only (MP3).

No! All content comes from YouTube — real songs, real shows, real creators. BabyBox is just the player, not a content generator. You pick exactly what your child can access.

You set a daily limit (by number of videos or total minutes). The LED strip turns yellow as a warning when the limit is close, then red when it's reached. Audio content is never limited.

Yes! Each figurine is independent. You can have different figurines for different children or different moods. The playback history tracks which figurines were used.

BabyBox will attempt to reconnect. If the speaker isn't available, audio won't play but video will still display on the TV (silent). A buzzer beep alerts that something's wrong.

The enclosure is 3D-printed with rounded corners and no small removable parts. The figurines are designed to be chunky and toddler-safe. All electronics are fully enclosed. The magnets are embedded inside the plastic.

Yes! The figurine base is standardized (35mm diameter with magnet and RFID pockets). You can design any character on top — we use Meshy AI to generate 3D models, then merge them with the base in Blender.
